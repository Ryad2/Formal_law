# Statement of Author Contribution and AI Use

Project: Formalizing Copyright Law through Melody Similarity

Course: Law and Computation II, EPFL

Authors: Ryad Mohamed Aouak, Romain Greub, Gresi Alejandra Velasco-Miranda, Zichun Zhou

## Author Contributions

The following contribution statement reflects the information currently confirmed by the group. The contribution split is equal: each author contributed approximately 25% of the overall project.

### Ryad Mohamed Aouak - 25%

Contributed to defining the project scope, research question, legal framing, and overall project direction; helped coordinate the division of tasks and alignment between the legal, computational, and presentation components; produced the full final poster and written report.

### Romain Greub - 25%

Contributed to data collection, case annotation, corpus construction, MIDI material collection, and methodology framing.

### Gresi Alejandra Velasco-Miranda - 25%

Contributed to MIDI preparation, melody extraction, sequence representation, alignment explanation, method overview, and result interpretation.

### Zichun Zhou - 25%

Contributed to global PMI, local PMI, and hybrid PMI scoring, score table generation, threshold interpretation, and computational result analysis.

All authors participated in reviewing selected cases and discussing whether the computational results were plausible in light of the legal and musical context. All authors reviewed or will review the final submitted materials and remain responsible for the content.

## AI Use

The authors used ChatGPT and Gemini for the following limited purposes:

- generating code for sequence representation and alignment, based on the authors' prior methodological decision to use bioinformatics-inspired sequence-alignment algorithms;
- poster and report layout support;
- poster and report visual organization support;
- language correction.

AI output was not treated as an independent legal, factual, or academic authority. All AI-assisted material was reviewed by the authors. The authors remain responsible for the final claims, legal interpretation, computational interpretation, citations, code, and submitted wording.
